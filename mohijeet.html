Mobirise

Enhancing experience for children visiting Assam Museum

The future lies in the hands of the young and I agree that this saying hits the nail on the head when it comes to children visiting museums. Children are never too young to learn about our cultural heritage and the collections that represent its physical manifestation. Museums simply need to be more enjoyable and more appealing to children. Therein lies the greatest potential. 

Game | visualization | interface | installation


Project by Mohijeet Das 

Guided by Mriganka Madhukaillya


Many people claim that museums are a place for children to have fun, yet museum visitor researchers are well aware that the fun experience often leads to learning outcomes. Learning knows no boundaries, and museums can be considered as a kind of learning landscapes for children. If learning is viewed as a process of change in knowledge, attitudes, and values, then museums provide an ideal and provocative learning environment. Learning in museum happen when children connect with interesting objects or experience. 

This project is an attempt to understand the different factors that influence children to experience in Assam State Museum and to design a solution that would provide a better learning and engaging experience to the children visiting the museum.
